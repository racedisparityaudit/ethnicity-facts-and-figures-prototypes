Crime, justice and the law

Overview

Use of stop and search among Black people has fallen since 2008/09 but remains higher than for other groups. Black people are also more likely to be more likely to be arrested, to be remanded in custody at Crown Court (rather than allowed out on bail) and to have lower levels of confidence in their local police than other ethnic groups.

The percentage of prosecutions resulting in conviction was highest among White defendants and lowest for Asians. Black and Asian offenders received the longest average custodial sentences at around 2 years compared to 18 months for White offenders.

These statistics do not take into account other factors such as type of offences, which may differ by ethnic group.
